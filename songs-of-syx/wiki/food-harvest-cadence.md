# Food strategy — harvest cadence over food type

The stability principle (community, #chat-and-discussions, 2026-07-06): **"1 harvest a year
is risky; harvest every day is not."** Food crises usually come from cadence, not volume —
a single annual grain harvest means one bad year or one planning error starves you;
fish/meat/hunting harvest continuously and self-buffer.

Early game: hunting is "super powerful" with 1–2 tech levels (community); v70 made hunters
effective longer but with diminishing returns per employee *(patch-note)*. Hunting caps you
at ~300 pop — see [[population-thresholds]]. Long term: the grain→bread pipeline is the
strong scaling play, now also trade-viable (see [[trade]]).

Anti-pattern in the wild: a player with 150 hours still hitting "1 food day / 3 starving" —
hours don't fix a cadence problem.

## History

- **2026-07-06 (game-data, dev manual Ch9 + ROOMS):** the cadence principle gets dev-stated backing — fisheries "low but constant" per worker; farms "produce food in bigger chunks... require more foresight and storage." Early-game reliability order per dev: **fisheries → pastures → farms → refiner chains**. Key mechanics: pastures use base fertility for animal *density* (worker count), NOT per-worker yield → viable on poor land, +output with fresh water; orchards out-produce farms per worker and per tile but start slow (alluvium best); hunter room caps ~**15 hunters per map** before decline; all foods equally nutritious — preference only affects satisfaction. Farms: 64 tiles/1 worker min → 2,025 tiles/~31 workers max. Sustainable at scale: farms, orchards, fisheries, pastures, refiners, cannibalism, trade, region taxes.
