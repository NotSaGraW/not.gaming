# Food strategy — harvest cadence over food type

The stability principle (community, #chat-and-discussions, 2026-07-06): **"1 harvest a year is risky; harvest every day is not."** Food crises usually come from cadence, not volume — a single annual grain harvest means one bad year or one planning error starves you; fish/meat/hunting harvest continuously and self-buffer.

Early game: hunting is "super powerful" with 1–2 tech levels (community); v70 made hunters effective longer but with diminishing returns per employee *(patch-note)*. Hunting caps you at ~300 pop — see [[population-thresholds]]. Long term: the grain→bread pipeline is the strong scaling play, now also trade-viable (see [[trade]]).

Anti-pattern in the wild: a player with 150 hours still hitting "1 food day / 3 starving" — hours don't fix a cadence problem.
